Introduction

The Cameroon flag has three equal vertical bands of green (hoist side), red, and yellow, with a yellow five-pointed start centered in the red band; vertical tricolor recalls the flag of France; red symbolizes unity, yellow the sun, happiness, and the savannahs in the north, and green hope and the forests south; the star is referred to as the "star of unity".

Geographic notes

Sometimes referred to as the hinge of Africa because of its central location on the continent and its position at the west-south juncture of the Gulf of Guinea; throughout the country there are areas of thermal springs and indications of current or prior volcanic activity; Mount Cameroon, the highest mountain in the Sub-Saharan west Africa, is an active volcano.
